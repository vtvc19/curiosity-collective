The Mission

Why this exists

Access to guidance shouldn't depend on which school you attend.

Curiosity Collective is an independent nonprofit built on a simple premise: students working on science fair projects and STEM research competitions produce stronger work when someone experienced is in their corner, pressure-testing a hypothesis, helping them make sense of a dense paper, or telling them a result is worth chasing further.

Mentorship shouldn't be something only well-resourced schools can offer their students.

Mentorship is provided entirely free, one-on-one, and open to students internationally. There is no tuition, no sponsorship pitch, and no cost to families at any point. The founder mentors personally, drawing on a research background to help students sharpen their questions, design better experiments, and prepare to present their work with confidence, at the fair, at nationals, or wherever the competition takes them.

It also doubles as a research effort in its own right: a year into the program, the aim is to understand what actually helps young researchers improve, and to build a model worth expanding.

How Mentorship Works

From question to competition

Step 01

Apply

Share your project idea (or the fact that you don't have one yet), your fair or competition, and your timeline. Takes a few minutes.

Step 02

Get matched

You're paired directly with the founder for ongoing, personal mentorship, not a rotating pool of volunteers.

Step 03

Do the work, together

Regular check-ins to shape your hypothesis, design your methodology, troubleshoot results, and prepare your presentation or paper.

Step 04

Compete

Walk into your fair or competition with a project you understand inside and out, and someone in your corner if it doesn't go as planned.
